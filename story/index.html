My Story
When I was, a young boy, my father took me into the city ... to see a marching band
He said: "Son, when you grow up, would you be the savior of the broken? The beaten and the damned? "
He said: "Will you, defeat them? your demons and all those none believers, the plans that they have made? "
Well these lines are from one of my favorite bands, "My Chemical Romance", now let's tell you more a bit more about me! ^_^
I was born on May the 13th 1995 in Tehran, Iran. (In case you don't actually know where Iran is, it's a quite big country in the middle east, right between Iraq and Afghanistan :D). We were living in this city for my whole life (or at least till now), a city of 15 million people. I went to primary and secondary school in a usual public school named "Komeil". I can remember I was always a nerd during my childhood (and some also say during my early adulthood years, too :D); I was graduated from primary and secondary school with a GPA of 20/20(A+).
For some reason I started my high school in the oldest high school of this city, Alborz High school (Formerly known as The Americans College) in the city center. This school turned my life quite upside down, I pursued math and physics, seriously, for the first time in my life; attended Math, Physics, Computer and Astronomy Olympiad and was able to get to the final round of National Math and Computer Olympiads. As a high school student I just loved Geometry, Differential Equations and Calculus! I'm sure you can ask from any of my teachers and they'll vouch for me quite passionately (oh I used to drive them crazy with my questions :D I couldn't help it much). My main goal and interest during this era was becoming a scientist, someone who can contribute to the existing mathematical knowledge and be outstanding, while doing it.
After the Konkur (it's the national universities' entrance exam, something like S.A.T) I was ranked 1000 between about 300,000 test takers. My love for the mathematics, coupled with a fascination for tech. lead me to where I am right now, studying Computer Engineering in Amirkabir University of Technology - The second best university of my country. Here I became fascinated with student activities that are there for the students and are also run by them, I became more and more drawn to the Student Scientific Chapter of our faculty by our seniors and eventually was elected as a member of it, for two consecutive terms. I found working as a member of our scientific chapter really amazing, it's comprised of a variety of tasks that I could never even dream of doing as a student, tasks like holding international contests, fairs and talks, communicating with the wide variety of industrial and academic people.
My academic studies started in the hardware engineering field. I passed my semesters with impressive scores (except the third one which I get to it in a moment). I actually love teaching, and this love, coupled with my good scores resulted in me being the teaching assistant of many of the courses. Through this experience I came to know the responsibilities of a good teacher and I could also use my ideas as a student to further improve my teaching methods and skills. Being a teaching assistant, also helped me to establish useful relationships with many members of our faculty and professors. I also participated in two main research projects, one of which is my final thesis; so I can tell I have quite a lot of educational experience to start with, it turns me into a quite good graduate candidate, something I'm trying to do my best to be better at.
As I was working passionately in the SSC, I was also pursuing my career goals, in order to fulfill my purpose in academic studies I needed to save as much money as it's possible, so I started working for different companies in the most accessible field at the time, Web and Mobile Development. I started in the summer after my third semester, working as an intern, learning iOS programming, Android programming and also PHP. Since I was quite more passionate about the UI/UX field, I started learning new front-end technologies that were data driven and reactive, I spent the next three years in this field of work, developing and implementing seamless user experience and interface. I worked in Fandogh, a startup made by a bunch of our department's students with the goal of developing a series of online services to address a wide variety of educational and scholar needs. Working in this startup was one of the best and most refreshing work experiences, I have, because we could work on the projects we like, with the novel technology we prefer and if that's not the dream then what is? :)
Unfortunately due to the financial problems in our country and some unfortunate university events, the startup experience didn't live long enough to reach its final goal. I was off to Snappfood, the biggest online food ordering company in my country. I started working there, developing their new front-end using latest technologies in React. I've been working there for almost a year right now, a place with wonderful people and a functional working process. I learnt the basics of team-work and working for a big corporation in this company. During the last months, though, I was quite obsessively working on my thesis project 24/7 and had to depart the company to pursue my academic life.
Well, now it's time to explain my downfall in the third semester of my studies. I was diagnosed with a stage-2 colon cancer, a piece of news that devastated me, my family and friends. That semester I was really struggling to keep my spirits up between multiple visits to hospitals and doctors and my educational tasks. I hit the bottom of my GPA during that semester but with the help of many good doctors, my wonderful friends and the support of my family I was able to pass through the crisis and am right now a healthy person, totally free of the cancer. It might have taken about 3 devastating years but I learned a lot about it and came out of it, a different person.
I always saw myself in an academic position, from the beginning of my primary school days, up until right now. I don't really think that I've been living up to my full academic potential in the past years and that's the reason I've been trying to change my route, take more online courses, participate in some active research in Computer Graphics, Game Development and HCI. I see myself in a professor's robe in a couple of years and I don't think anything or anyone can stop me from getting there :) so, now that you know almost everything about me, let me tell you more about my hobbies.
My biggest hobby and obsession from my early years were two things, solving math and gaming! yep! I'm a gamer and I'm proud to be one! I don't think that games are for kids, or that they're a simple waste of time, games are just novel ways of telling a story, experiencing the lives you couldn't live and trying the things you can't be even thinking of doing in the real life; in a sentence, games are awesome! (of course I don't mean mind-numbing casual mobile games that make you itch and spend every moment of your life :D). Math on the other hand, don't you really think it's amazing that we can model the world around us using a series of rules, measure it and then solve it using them? if you don't, then sorry my friend because you're quite dead inside :D nothing could ever bring me the satisfaction of solving a calculus or geometry problem after two weeks of thinking and struggling.
I also listen to music quite a lot! and my taste in it is quite ... well ... quite odd :D I do really enjoy classic works of Violin, Piano and their duets, But I also love some proper rock band music like Green Day, My Chemical Romance and likes of so. I think music is the best way to capture a moment in time, in your life, so I have a playlist in Spotify named "Stories", be sure to check it out, it's the story of my life :)
I hope you've found what you came here for :D whatever it might have been! I hope your questions about me were answered in a way, but I also hope you have now even more questions, questions that make you contact me and ask me anything you want :) don't be shy, my contact is available in the Contact section. Be sure to check it out.
